PhD graduate in biomedical engineering and front-end developer

I am a PhD student in biomedical science researching surface forces at the biological-material interface. My doctoral research consists of measuring the water-induced forces at biological interfaces using an atomic force microscope. The aim is to design bioinert surfaces with structured interfacial water for biomedical and biosensing applications.

On the side, I enjoy developing simple web applications and (more recently) explore the application of prediction algorithms to medical technologies. Either through the development of communication apps or constructing intelligent analytical methods, I believe that healthcare technologies can be improved using emerging tech trends such as cloud services and/or machine learning. I enjoy coding and revealing the scientific meaning of data, and my professional goal is to integrate biomedicine research with tech for smart healthcare products.
